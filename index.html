Get to Know the Big Dipper!

Dubhe: The brightest star in the Big Dipper, Dubhe is a giant orange star and serves as a pointer toward Polaris, the North Star.
Merak: A white subgiant star, Merak, along with Dubhe, forms the line that points to Polaris in the night sky.
Phecda: A young, hot, bluish-white star, Phecda marks the lower corner of the Big Dipper’s bowl.
Megrez: The faintest of the seven, Megrez is a white main-sequence star that connects the bowl of the Dipper to its handle.
Alioth: The brightest in the Big Dipper’s handle, Alioth is a white giant and exhibits unusual magnetic behavior.
Mizar: Famous for being a binary star, Mizar forms a striking visual double with its companion, Alcor, in the handle of the Big Dipper.
Alkaid: A blue giant star, Alkaid marks the end of the Big Dipper’s handle and is one of the hottest and youngest stars in the group.
